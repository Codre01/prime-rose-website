About Us

Prime Rose (Montessori) School ("Prime Rose") was founded in September 1986 by Mrs. A.O. Joseph, who established a legacy spanning nearly 40 years. Located in the serene neighborhood of Gbagada Estate, Phase II, Lagos, Nigeria, the school is dedicated to nurturing each child’s potential from an early age and guiding them to achieve their fullest capabilities. Our motto, “Learning to Learn,” emphasizes equipping pupils with the tools to apply their knowledge effectively, fostering balanced and joyful personalities. Prime Rose is committed to delivering quality education and enriching experiences that support cognitive, physical, and emotional growth. Our mission is to build strong connections with children, nurturing them through everyday care and learning. In doing so, we aim to develop unique individuals who grow into the leaders of tomorrow. Our vision is to create happy, unforgettable memories for every child during their formative years with us.

We focus on:

  • Teamwork: Collaborating as a unified community.
  • Balanced Curriculum: Offering a comprehensive educational program.
  • Progression: Ensuring consistent growth across key stages.
  • Inclusivity: Catering to diverse abilities.
  • Independence: Promoting confidence and self-reliance.
  • Curiosity: Encouraging enthusiasm, creativity, and logical thinking.
  • Respect: Fostering personal self-esteem and mutual respect in a stimulating and challenging environment.

Our Experience

Over the years, parental expectations regarding education have significantly shifted, particularly in response to the decline of the public education system. In the 1980s, parents largely valued schools that emphasized academic content and upheld high moral standards. At the time, public secondary schools were still functional and widely utilized by Nigerian families.

However, these expectations have evolved. Today, parents seek schools that combine strong academic content with high-quality offerings, including aesthetics, spacious environments, and diverse extracurricular activities. As Nigeria’s macro economy and middle class continue to expand, the demand for quality private education is expected to grow. Prime Rose School aims to position itself as a leader in this space by focusing on both academic excellence and quality, making it the preferred choice for parents and an attractive workplace for qualified teachers

Key Management
Prime Rose School operates a structure that is highly sensitive to the dynamics of running a school. Due to Prime Rose School’s commitment to providing a conducive learning environment for kids to learn life skills and develop strong characters, the School insists on experienced teachers with very high standards. The school is presently being administered by a Governing Board made up of 4 individuals who are responsible for setting the broad strategy of the school. The Proprietress is responsible for the day to day running of the school and is being supported by a capable Head Teacher that supervises the core teaching and non-teaching staff.

Kolapo Joseph

Kolapo Joseph is an astute investment banker and financial services expert with over two decades of experience. He holds a degree in Finance from University of Maryland, College Park, U.S.A and MBA from the University of Chicago, Booth School of Business. Kolapo started his career at Vetiva Capital Management (one of Nigeria’s leading independent investment banks) where he joined as a pioneer staff and rose to the Deputy Head of Investment Banking Division. He was the pioneer Head of the Capital Markets and Advisory Unit when the company won several investment bank industry awards. In 2011, Kolapo joined United Capital Plc. formerly UBA Capital Plc. During his role as the Director and Head, Project and Infrastructure Finance at United Capital Plc, he successfully built the Project and Infrastructure Division to one of the leading (top 3) energy and infrastructure teams in Nigeria. Raising over US$10 billion for clients in West and Central Africa. In his current capacity as the Chief, Corporate Finance and Development Officer at North South Power Company Limited, Kolapo is responsible for developing and driving the company’s strategy and finance related activities. Owing to his experience and exploits in the last 20 years, Kolapo Joseph sits on the board of the following companies:

  1. Strom Infrastructure Investment & Management Limited as a Director
  2. CEC Africa Investments as a Director
  3. Emerging Africa Capital as a Director
  4. Nigerian Electricity Supply Corporation Limited, Jos (The oldest indigenous integrated utility power company in Nigeria) as a Director
  5. Coronation Infrastructure Fund (member, advisory board)
  6. Treasures Microfinance Bank as a Director
  7. Prime Rose School as a member of the governing board

Wola Joseph Condotti

Wola Joseph Condotti is the Group MD/CEO of West Power & Gas Limited, the parent company of Eko Electricity Distribution PLC (EKEDP) and six other affiliate companies with interests in both conventional and renewable energy sectors. In addition to this role, she serves as the Director of the Power and Renewable Division at the Women in Energy Network (WIEN) in a non-executive capacity. Prior to her current role, Wola was the pioneer Chief, Legal & Company Secretariat at EKEDP. During her tenure, she also held multiple key positions including Head of Regulatory Compliance, Chief Human Resources and Administration Officer, Supervising Chief of the Customer Service Department, and Data Protection Officer. Her previous experience includes roles as General Counsel/Company Secretariat Lagoon Home Savings and Loans, and Legal Associate at Banwo & Ighodalo. Wola holds a law degree from the University of Ibadan, an LLM in International Finance Law from Harvard Law School, and an MBA from INSEAD Business School. She is also an International Finance Corporation (World Bank) Board Evaluation Certified Professional and Corporate Governance Trainer. Wola's achievements have been recognized through numerous awards, including being listed among the top 100 General Counsels by the Legal 500 GC Powerlist: Nigeria, 2024. She was also honored as the "INSEADer of the Month" in April 2024 by INSEAD Business School and named one of the top 100 most inspiring women in Africa by the Enterprising Women Awards Africa, 2024. Additionally, she was ranked 11th among the top 50 General Counsels in Nigeria in the 2019 edition of the LKS Seminar Legal Magazine and was recognized by Legal Blitz in 2018 as one of the top 40 under 40 lawyers significantly impacting the legal profession in Nigeria. Most recently, she was listed among 50 of Africa’s most inspirational female energy executives by Africa Oil Week (AOW). Wola serves on numerous boards, providing advisory guidance on corporate and business matters. Her board memberships include the Women in Energy Network, Prime-Rose Nursery & Primary School, Jimi Tewe Foundation, Newswire Law & Events Magazine, the Electricity Law Reports (the first ever Electricity Law Report in Africa), and the Public Affairs & Advocacy Committee of the Lagos Chamber of Commerce & Industry. She is a member of the Institute of Directors and the Society for Corporate Governance Nigeria. Additionally, she is an associate member of the Institute of Chartered Secretaries and Administrators of Nigeria, and Women in Management, Business, and Public Service.

Mrs. A.O. Joseph

Mrs. A.O. Joseph is the proprietress/founder of Prime Rose School. She is a B.Sc. holder of the prestigious University of Lagos, and has prior to establishing the Prime Rose School, taught Economics at various levels for many years. Mrs. Joseph began her teaching career at New Era Secondary School in Surulere in 1976 before moving to Methodist Girls High School in 1980 where she rose to become the head of the Economics Department. In 1986, she established Prime Rose School and has driven the school to its current reputation and success.  She takes pride in the school’s Alumni who have become successful leaders all over the world in their various professions. Mrs. Joseph is well respected and an admired voice in the education community and continues to channel her efforts towards ensuring that educational standards and quality education remain relevant.
